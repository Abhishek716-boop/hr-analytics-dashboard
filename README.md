# HR Insights & Attrition Analytics Dashboard

An end-to-end data analysis project focused on understanding employee turnover, identifying critical attrition drivers, and providing actionable insights to improve workforce retention.

---

## 📊 Project Overview
This project analyzes HR data from a workforce of **1,416 employees** to understand why **229 employees left** (an overall attrition rate of **16.2%**). By examining variables like age, salary, job roles, and tenure, the dashboard highlights key risk zones that human resource teams need to address.

## 🔑 Key Insights & Findings

* **The Age Risk Zone:** Mid-career professionals aged **26–35** account for the highest turnover, with **111 departures**.
* **Salary Correlation:** Low earners are highly vulnerable. Employees with a salary **Upto 5k** represent nearly 70% of total attrition (158 departures).
* **High-Churn Roles:** **Laboratory Technicians** (60) and **Sales Executives** (55) are the top two roles experiencing maximum churn.
* **The "Year 1" Critical Peak:** Attrition peaks sharply at **Year 1** of service, indicating a strong need for better onboarding or early-stage engagement.

---

## 💡 Actionable Recommendations for HR
1. **Revamp Entry-Level Compensation:** Review the pay structure for the under-5k salary bracket to control mass departures.
2. **First-Year Retention Program:** Introduce structured mentorship or 30-60-90 day check-ins to flatten the Year 1 attrition spike.
3. **Role-Specific Interventions:** Investigate burnout or commission structures in Laboratory and Sales departments.
